UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH
PRODUCTS
  • Undergrads sometimes the BEST at data entry
  • Yet undergrads should not be grunt work, more than rote, skills based
  • Skills taught should be broadly applicable in academia or the private sector (Tableau)
  • DS offers undergrads a way to be direct contributors in a way few other fields do, making them stakeholders
  • GitHub and static site hosting gives ownership of code to undergrads beyond current class
  • Projects should consider how they can meaningfully create undergrad spinoff research
  • Undergrads can create related research projects that drive digital footprints

ETHICAL DIGITAL CITIZENS
PRODUCTS
  • Students don't just learn data, but data crticism and literacy
  • Offers them tools to think about the potentials for algorithmic inequity
  • Students need data civics, and to understand the role data has in shaping national and international discourses. And how their own private data plays a role in shaping this system.
  • We have all become aware of the power of tech, and graduates of Haverford will surely be among those in the room making future. For a school with an emphasis on its honor code, producing lasting ethical digital citizens should be a boon.
BUILDING MOMENTUM
  • Ultimately, I am talking about building positive feedback loops. By linking people and products via processes we can build gains over time. Students and faculty lead to more projects, lead to more students and faculty. This is how we build institutional memory and momentum.
  • And if you are thinking all these courses, applications, and workbooks are all too much for one person, I say to you: that is surely my point. That is why processes are key. Whatever I think of my own skills, no one person has enough hours in the day to do it all. But, a core of key individuals can act as centers of gravity and propel the orbits of surrounding entities. Thank you very much for you time.
